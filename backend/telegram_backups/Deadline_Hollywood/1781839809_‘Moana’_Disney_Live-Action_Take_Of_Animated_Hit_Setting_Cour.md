# ‘Moana’: Disney Live-Action Take Of Animated Hit Setting Course For $85M U.S. Opening – Box Office Early Look

## Article Content

![Article Image](https://deadline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Moana-2.jpg?w=681&h=383&crop=1)

Disney’s live-action take of its 2016 animated hit Moana is looking at an $85M North American opening when it bows on July 10, according to three-week pre-release tracking Thursday morning.

Dwayne Johnson returns in the flesh to play Maui, the demigod he portrayed as a cartoon. Catherine Laga’aia takes over as Moana, that protag previously played by Auli’i Cravalho in both Moana and Moana 2. Fosse/Verdon director Thomas Kail is helming the live-action Moana.

If some are shocked by the lower forecast now on Moana, take note: Yes, there’s the tried-and-true excuse that it’s still early in the campaign, but also you have Toy Story 5 ($140M-$150M+ outlook this weekend) and Illumination/Universal’s Minions & Monsters eyeing at a bare minimum a $95M 5-day opening when it invades on Wednesday, July 1. In addition, that projection on Moana is on par with Uni’s live-action take of DreamWorks Animation’s How to Train Your Dragon, which began with $84.6M last year.

## Watch on Deadline

Best first choice for Moana in general audiences with women over 25, above Mufasa: The Lion King ($35.4M in 2024) and How to Train Your Dragon but way behind Moana 2, which was an enormous Thanksgiving hit holding the 5-day record start of $225.4M back in 2024 ($139.7M 3-day). The original Moana in 2016 posted a 5-day opening over Thanksgiving of $82M ($56.5M 3-day). Moms and dads are even on taking their kids under 12 to see Moana, but again that’s way behind versus Moana 2 when that hit tracking and ahead of Mufasa and How to Train Your Dragon. Girls under 12 have the best first choice with Moana, well behind Lilo & Stitch and Moana 2 when they hit tracking but ahead of Mufasa and How to Train Your Dragon.
