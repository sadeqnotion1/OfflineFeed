## Cindy Crawford

Cindy Crawford speaks out againt anti-semitism while being honored with the Torch of Liberty Award at the 2026 ADL National Home Furnishings Industry Awards Dinner at Chelsea Piers in New York City.

“Growing up in a place as homogenous as DeKalb, Illinois, I was admittedly sheltered in many ways. But I also wasn’t raised around much prejudice or discrimination,” she said, in part. “To me, antisemitism felt like a relic from the past, something confined to history books. And then came Oct. 7.

Crawford continued, “What I saw afterward, especially online, was heartbreaking. I continue to be saddened, honestly shocked, by the antisemitic rhetoric that so quickly emerged from the shadows across social media.”

She also said that she and her husband Rady Gerber never “imagined a world where our children would see people condemned simply because they are Jewish. I am proud to stand beside my Jewish friends, my Jewish husband, my Jewish family. And by doing that, I am also saying, ‘No – no to antisemitism, no to hatred, no to dehumanizing people because of who they are.’”
