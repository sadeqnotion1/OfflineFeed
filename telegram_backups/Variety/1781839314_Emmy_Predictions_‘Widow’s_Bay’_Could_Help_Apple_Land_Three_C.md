Primetime Emmys Predictions Commentary All Categories (Updated: June 18, 2026): The Television Academy announced June 16 that all categories for the 78th Emmy Awards nominations will be unveiled on Wednesday, July 8, including the nine categories previously slated for July 15, such as cinematography, music and sound editing. The move means complete nomination totals for programs and networks will be available immediately following the announcement. Emmy winners Liza Colón-Zayas (“The Bear”) and Jeff Hiller (“Somebody Somewhere”) will present the nominations on July 8.

With only four days left in the voting window, there are small tweaks to the predictions below and on the charts. “The Pitt” added two mentions in the supporting drama races (Gerran Howell and Isa Briones), which pushes the series to a projected 21 noms, and second-most of the lot, behind “Pluribus,” looking solid for possibly 24 noms.

Apple’s “Widow’s Bay” is the other big mover, landing in the comedy series, lead actor comedy and supporting actress comedy slots, pushing it up to 10 noms as it’s in the cultural zeitgeist.

The ranking charts for writing, directing and short form have also debuted.

The finish line is close.

Nomination voting runs June 11-22. Final-round voting takes place Aug. 17-26 ahead of the Creative Arts Emmy Awards and Governors Gala on Sept. 5-6. The Primetime Emmy Awards will air Sept. 14 on NBC.
