# TelevisaUnivison Lures Media-Buying Veteran Mike Law as EVP, Brand Partnerships

## Article Content

![Article Image](https://variety.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/Mike-Michael-Law-2026-1-e1781814188350.png?w=1000&h=667&crop=1)

Spanish-language broadcaster TelevisaUnivison has hired Michael Law, a longtime media-buying executive who was most recently CEO of Dentsu’s Carat North America, as its new executive vice president of brand partnerships.

He will report to John Kozack, who was recently named to lead ad sales for the company.

Law has more than 25 years working for prominent media buying agencies. Before taking the CEO job at Carat North America, he led investment for Dentsu in the U.S. and spent time on Pfizer’s media team. He first joined Dentsu in 2000, overseeing national TV buying across important accounts. Law got his start working for Arnold, a prominent agency based in Boston.

The hires of Kozack and Law suggest new interest by top executives at TelevisaUnivision in relying on veterans familiar with the sales space. Kozack replaced Tim Natividad, who had previous experience at Roku and Amazon.

The company’s advertising revenue in the fourth quarter came to $856 million, flat with results from the previous year. In the U.S., advertising revenue declined 11% to $423 million.
