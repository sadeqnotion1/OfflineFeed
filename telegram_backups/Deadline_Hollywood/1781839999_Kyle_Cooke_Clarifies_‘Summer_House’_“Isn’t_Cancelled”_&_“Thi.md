Kyle Cooke of Bravo‘s Summer House had many people thinking he had quit the show after 10 seasons.

However, in an edit to his original social media post, the DJ and entrepreneur is explaining that the show is not canceled.

“End of Season 10 and a decade of filming this show as I know it. The show isn’t cancelled and this is not me announcing I won’t return,” Cooke said in an edit to his Instagram post. “I was feeling emo and always planned sharing the cover photo because it DID feel like the end of an era, which is why everyone else was so emotional saying their goodbyes on the finale. Sorry everyone!”

Cooke shared a post on social media looking back at his last day of filming Season 10 of Summer House last year. Viewers who watched the Season 10 finale saw the cast very emotional as they ended their summer, with some embarking on the spinoff In the City. With the tenth season of the Bravo reality series officially ending this past week, Cooke dug into the nostalgia and shared his feelings in an emotional post. Watch on Deadline

“I can’t tell how hard it was to muster up a smile in this photo with my producers. Per usual I was the last to leave the house when we wrapped because I lug so much gear out. But this year was different,” Cooke shared. “10 seasons in the books. A new show on the way. And a heartbreaking way to end one helluva run. I’m tearing up as I type this because we truly become a family shooting this show and it was a gut wrenching way to leave this house for good.”

Many Summer House fans took this as Cooke saying goodbye to the series, but he further explained that was not the case.

Summer House usually starts filming on the 4th of July, which is fast approaching. Many things seem bound to change following the scandal that rocked the show’s cast this year. Westling Wilson, who was in the middle of the scandal, is reported not to be returning to the series for Season 11 after starting a relationship with Cooke’s estranged wife, Amanda Batula, who is also a cast member on Summer House.
