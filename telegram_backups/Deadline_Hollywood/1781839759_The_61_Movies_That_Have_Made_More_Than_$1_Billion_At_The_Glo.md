## Article Content

![Article Image](https://deadline.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/billion-gallery.jpg?w=681&h=383&crop=1)

Universal reached a major milestone in early June when Illumination and Nintendo’s animated sequel The Super Mario Galaxy Movie crossed the $1 billion mark at the global box office. It became the 61st film to hit the three-comma threshold, including its predecessor The Super Mario Bros Movie in 2023.

RELATED: The Top-Grossing Movies At The Box Office Every Year Since 1977

Among the elite list of billion-dollar-grossing films are sequels, Disney remakes and iconic franchises such as Star Wars, Avatar, James Bond, The Lord of the Rings, the Marvel Cinematic Universe, Harry Potter, The Fast & The Furious, Jurassic Park, The Dark Knight, Transformers and Disney and Pixar toons.

The 2023 summer smash Barbie, starring Margot Robbie and Ryan Gosling, propelled Greta Gerwig into the rankings as the first solo female director to reach the 10-digit milestone. Jennifer Lee hit the billion level with Disney’s Frozen II (2019), co-directing alongside Chris Buck.
